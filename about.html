About - Jay You

Kudakwashe William Rukuni (born October 4, 1994), who performs under the name Jay You, is a Zimbabwean trap / hip hop artist / musician and lead member of the band South Samora. He is popularly referred to by fans and media as "Judge", the English word for "Mutongi Gava", a name he earned mostly because of his signature intellectual capabilities of solving disputes. The name "Jay You" comes from his actions, "Mutongi", which means "Judge".

Kudakwashe William Rukuni is the last born in a family of three. As a young boy he liked writing, reading his father's dissertations, swimming in the river with friends, and hunting in the forests. Time and again he would come back home with his shorts torn from all the climbing and adventure. Most importantly, he liked to sing. He nurtured this habit through singing at school assemblies and at church, as well as playing the guitar with his teacher Lucas Tavaya (Lrd Mega). As he grew older and as his guitar skills improved, Jay You felt confident and inspired to pursue his interests in music. People began to realize his natural gift in music and language, and encouraged him to be serious with it.

He attended Chishawasha Primary School and later Visitation Makumbi High School in Harare in Mashonaland East Province. He then relocated to Harare where he stayed with his family and eventually completed his degree Harare at Catholic University Of Zimbabwe.

Music Career

Jay You's first experience with composing music was during his early years of high school. He started to record rnb and rap tracks with his friends with some tracks creating a vibe in the suburb, but failing to hit the national music scene. He later recorded trap music and afro hip hop with Lrd Mega and other local producers, releasing singles such as "MaLame Niggarz" and "Vizzy Legends".

He started his career in 2010. In 2012 he released his first commercially launched album, Toast To The Winner and won a Toast Masters Association Award for song of the Year for the song. He went on to release two more albums in 2014, Vizzy Legends and South Samora vol 2 (2015). The latter was commercially successful and contained the singles "Distant Lover" and "Calling Home", the latter of which featured TreyXL. Cypher was commercially successful, and he was offered a contract for the Wednesday slots at a local Harare pub called Basement.

In 2016 he released his fourth album Judgment Night 3 "Lean In My Kup". In 2017 he released Highway Music.

In 2019 he opened a new studio and released his sixth studio album, Jay You LP.

His most notable work was a collaboration with award-winning China based artist De Troy on his hit song "Bounce", which reached over a thousand views on Youtube in less than a month after its release. "Bounce" has enjoyed massive airplay on MTV Base and Trace Africa.

On 2 November 2020, Jay You launched his eighth studio album titled PLP. The album features collaborations from well known African artists such as Takura and AReece. In 2020, Jay You was nominated for the Best New Comer Artist in Southern Africa in the All Africa Music Awards (AFRIMAs).
